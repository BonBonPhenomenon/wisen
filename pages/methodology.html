Research methodology

How Wisen researches

Every Wisen report is produced using a consistent research methodology: structured web research across live NZ and AU sources, reviewed and verified by Bonnie Vujcich before publication. This page documents how that process works, what the data quality levels mean, and where each report's figures come from.

The process

Research, then review

Wisen uses AI-assisted research to gather pricing data from publicly available NZ and AU sources -- agency websites, published pricing pages, industry benchmark reports, and platform data. That research is then reviewed by Bonnie Vujcich, who has 20 years of experience across NZ and AU marketing, before any report is published.

The AI does the searching. The human does the verifying. Nothing is published without that editorial review step. That is not a disclaimer -- it is how the research process is designed.

Each report notes the period during which its research was conducted. Marketing service pricing changes. Always check the research date before using any figure for budget planning.

Source standards

Where the data comes from

Every data point in every Wisen report is sourced according to a strict hierarchy. Higher tiers are always preferred over lower tiers.

1
Agency and provider pricing pages
Pricing published directly by the agency or freelancer on their own website. The most reliable source -- the provider is stating what they charge.
2
Published industry benchmark reports
Named, dated reports with a stated methodology -- IAB NZ, IAB Australia, IDNZ, The Creative Store, Aquent, and equivalents.
3
Platform-published data
Data published by Google, Meta, and equivalent platforms directly. Treated as primary for benchmark CPC, CPM, and CTR figures.
4
Aggregated benchmark reports
Reports from tools such as WordStream, AgencyAnalytics, and equivalents that aggregate data from multiple sources. Useful for context, lower confidence than primary sources.
5
Estimated or extrapolated figures
Where primary data is unavailable, figures may be extrapolated from comparable market data. Always flagged clearly as Estimated with the methodology noted.
Data quality

What the confidence levels mean

Every data point in a Wisen report carries a confidence level. Here is what each one means.

Confirmed
Taken directly from a published primary source -- an agency pricing page, an industry benchmark report, or platform-published data. The figure exists exactly as stated in a named, dated, publicly accessible source.
Estimated
Extrapolated from comparable market data using a stated methodology. For example: an NZ figure derived from AU data with a market discount applied. The methodology is always noted alongside the figure.
Indicative
General market sense drawn from multiple sources, not attributable to a single confirmed figure. Useful for context and direction. Not suitable for precise budget planning without further validation.
NZ data

The NZ data challenge

NZ-specific primary data is structurally thinner than AU for most marketing service categories. Fewer NZ agencies publish pricing. Fewer NZ-specific benchmark reports exist. This is a known market condition, not a research failure.

How NZ gaps are handled: Where NZ-specific primary data is unavailable, AU data is used as a proxy with a market discount applied and the methodology noted explicitly. NZ CPCs run approximately 14% below the US average -- confirmed via Web Antler NZ 2026 -- and NZ pricing generally runs slightly below AU for comparable services. Any figure derived this way is marked Estimated, not Confirmed.

Wisen never presents an AU figure as an NZ figure. Where NZ-specific data could not be found, that is stated directly in the report.
